2020 APSA West Regional Meeting



“Intersectional Identities in an Intersectional Career”
Hosted by the University of Arizona College of Medicine – Tucson & Phoenix Campuses

Join us virtually for student oral and poster presentations, trainee and physician-scientist panels, breakout sessions for trainees at every level, exciting faculty research presentations, and more! Undergraduate, medical and dual-degree students are highly encouraged to participate by presenting their research. Abstracts are due November 5th.

When: Saturday, December 5th, 2020
8:15am – 6:00pm

Where: Virtual

Who Should Attend?
Anyone interested in biomedical research is invited to attend. In particular, MD/PhD students, DO/PhD students, MD or DO students, PhD students, undergraduates, and high school students interested in medicine and biomedical research are encouraged to participate.

Registration Information
Registration will be available on this site from October 23rd until December 1st, 2020. You are not required to have an APSA account for registration. For problems or issues with registration please email (mailto:kendramarr@email.arizona.edu).

Cancellation Policy
Cancellations may be sent via email to (mailto:kendramarr@email.arizona.edu). The American Physician-Scientists Association reserves the right to cancel any program and assumes no responsibility for personal expenses.

Registration Costs
FREE FOR ALL REGISTRANTS. The University of Arizona College of Medicine is committed to having all interested students attend the conference without the extra burden of registration cost. Regardless of whether or not you are an APSA member, there will be no registration fees for this conference.

Abstracts
The APSA West Regional Meeting will highlight and reward the outstanding achievements of our undergraduate, graduate and medical student researchers. We encourage all high school, medical, graduate, and undergraduate students attending the meeting to submit an abstract. Please submit your abstract as early as possible to ensure the best chance for it to be selected for an oral presentation.

TO SUBMIT: Fill out abstract submission form upon registration
Deadline: November 5, 2020
Eligibility: Any attendee to the APSA West Regional Meeting is encouraged to submit an abstract proposing a poster and/or oral presentation
Abstract guidelines: Abstracts should be a maximum of 250 words. Abstracts should provide a brief description of research objectives, methodology, and summary of results and conclusions. Please do not include any figures, bibliographies or footnotes.
Presentation Type: Applicants may indicate if they wish to be considered for a poster presentation, oral presentation, or both. Only the top abstracts will be selected for oral presentations. Applicants will be notified in November the type(s) of presentations they have been selected for. Abstracts will be judged on impact, innovation, and clarity.
Oral Presentations: The top abstracts will be selected for oral presentations. Presentors will have 10 minutes to present the highlights of their work, followed by 2 to 3 minutes of questions from the audience. These presentations should focus on the overall impact of the research and should emphasize “the big picture” of the findings. Poster Presentations: Presentors will have 5 minutes to present the highlights of their work, followed by 2 to 3 minutes of questions from the audience.
